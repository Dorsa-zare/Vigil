What is Atr-e Shahr?

This project began with a personal observation. After returning to my hometown in Iran after a long time away, I noticed how much had changed — and realized there was no way to capture that feeling. The news covers events, maps show geography, but neither preserves what a place actually feels like to the people who live in it, love it, or had to leave it.

Vigil asks: can an AI system, fed enough human memory, produce something that feels true to a place? Everyone who leaves a memory here — diaspora, resident, visitor — contributes to a collective portrait of Persepolis that shifts and deepens over time.

The goal is to preserve the essence of a place from the people who felt it — not from whoever controls the narrative.
