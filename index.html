What is Atr-e Shahr?

Atr-e Shahr means "the perfume of the city" in Farsi, the invisible essence a place leaves on the people who have known it.

This project began with a personal observation. After returning to my hometown in Iran after a long time away, I noticed how much had changed and realized there was no way to capture that feeling. The news covers events, maps show geography, but neither preserves what a place actually feels like to the people who live in it, love it, or had to leave it. When cities go through conflict, protest, or slow transformation, the emotional record disappears. What remains is political coverage, not human memory.

Atr-e Shahr is a platform where anyone, diaspora, resident, visitor, or someone who simply loves a place, can contribute a short memory, feeling, or observation. These fragments are read by an AI that extracts their sensory and emotional content, building them into a collective prompt. An image AI then paints a portrait of that place from those accumulated memories, updating gradually as new voices arrive.

Over time you can watch the portrait shift, shaped not by cameras or official narratives, but by what people actually felt standing there. The goal is to preserve the essence of a place from the people who felt it.

Can an AI system, fed enough human memory, produce something that feels true to a place? That is the question this project is trying to answer.
